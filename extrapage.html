Most people can enhance their gut health naturally through diet. Here are the types of food that provide significant benefits to gut health.

High-Fiber Foods Like Beans, Oats, and Fruits

Fiber, a type of carbohydrate found in plant-based foods, is categorized as either soluble or insoluble.

Soluble fiber absorbs water and forms a gel that is consumed by gut bacteria, explains Alicia Romano, a clinical dietitian at Tufts Medical Center in Boston and spokesperson for the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics. On the other hand, insoluble fiber passes through your digestive tract largely intact, adding bulk to your stool. “This makes food pass more quickly through the GI tract, thus promoting regular bowel movements,” says Romano.

Both types of fiber aid in digestion and help prevent constipation. Consuming high-fiber foods also helps protect against weight gain and chronic conditions such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and certain types of cancer, according to a review in The Lancet.

Good sources of fiber include:

  • Beans, dried peas, and lentils
  • Bran (oat and wheat)
  • Dried fruits, such as prunes and raisins
  • Foods made with whole grains, such as whole-grain bread, whole-grain cereal, and whole-grain pasta
  • Whole grains, such as barley, quinoa, bulgur, and brown rice
  • Fresh fruits, especially apples with skin, pears with skin, oranges, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries, and strawberries
  • Nuts
  • Seeds
  • Vegetables, especially artichokes, broccoli, green peas, winter squash, and white potatoes and sweet potatoes with skin

Probiotic Foods Like Kimchi, Kombucha, and Kefir

Probiotic foods (such as kimchi, kombucha, and kefir) are rich in beneficial live microorganisms. Incorporating them into your diet is an effective way to enhance gut health, says Ryan Andrews, a principal nutritionist and advisor at Precision Nutrition, a digital coaching and certification company. “Fermented plant foods, in particular, appear to support gut health and offer various benefits,” he says. For instance, a 2021 study in the journal Cell found that participants who consumed fermented foods experienced increased microbiome diversity and improved immune responses.

A Varied Diet Improves Gut Health Naturally

Eating a diverse diet that includes different types of food is one of the best things you can do for your gut, says Andrews. “By consuming various foods, we provide different species [of bacteria] with different types of fuel,” he explains. “This can lead to greater microbial diversity and a more robust gut microbiome.” This is important because increased gut microbiome diversity is linked to better health outcomes.

A varied diet can include:

  • Eating foods from all five food groups, including fruits, vegetables, grains, protein foods, and dairy or fortified soy alternatives
  • Eating different types of fiber, such as from beans, whole grains, and vegetables
  • Eating a range of plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, whole grains, and legumes

In addition to eating a varied diet, there are other ways to promote a healthy gut, such as:

  • Staying hydrated
  • Engaging in regular physical activity
  • Managing stress
  • Getting enough sleep
